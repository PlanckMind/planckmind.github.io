AI Development Timeline

The journey of artificial intelligence from conceptual beginnings to modern achievements

1950

Turing Test Proposed

Alan Turing publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," introducing the Turing Test as a measure of machine intelligence.

Learn More
1956

Birth of AI at Dartmouth

The Dartmouth Workshop, organized by John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, and others, coins the term "Artificial Intelligence" and establishes it as a field of study.

Learn More
1966

ELIZA - First Chatbot

Joseph Weizenbaum creates ELIZA, an early natural language processing computer program that simulated conversation by pattern matching.

Learn More
1986

Backpropagation Revolution

The backpropagation algorithm for training multi-layer neural networks is rediscovered and popularized, enabling modern deep learning.

Learn More
1997

Deep Blue vs Kasparov

IBM's Deep Blue becomes the first computer system to defeat a reigning world chess champion under regular time controls.

Learn More
2011

IBM Watson Wins Jeopardy!

IBM's question-answering system defeats former Jeopardy! champions Brad Rutter and Ken Jennings.

Learn More
2014

Generative Adversarial Networks

Ian Goodfellow introduces GANs, enabling AI systems to generate realistic images, audio, and video content.

Learn More
2017

Transformer Architecture

Google researchers introduce the Transformer architecture, which becomes the foundation for modern large language models.

Learn More
2022

ChatGPT Revolution

OpenAI releases ChatGPT to the public, demonstrating unprecedented language understanding and generation capabilities.

Learn More
2024

Multimodal AI Systems

AI systems like GPT-4 and Gemini demonstrate sophisticated understanding across text, images, audio, and video.

Learn More

AI Learning Resources

Essential materials to deepen your understanding of artificial intelligence

Books

Foundational texts on AI history, theory, and applications:

  • Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach by Stuart Russell and Peter Norvig
  • Deep Learning by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, and Aaron Courville
  • The Master Algorithm by Pedro Domingos

Online Courses

Structured learning paths for beginners to advanced practitioners:

  • Machine Learning by Andrew Ng (Coursera)
  • Deep Learning Specialization by Andrew Ng
  • Practical Deep Learning for Coders (fast.ai)

Research Papers

Seminal papers that shaped the field of AI:

  • Deep Learning (Nature, 2015) by Yann LeCun, Yoshua Bengio, and Geoffrey Hinton
  • Attention Is All You Need (Transformer architecture)
  • Generative Adversarial Networks by Ian Goodfellow

Documentaries

Films exploring AI's impact on society and future:

  • AlphaGo - The story of DeepMind's AI defeating the world Go champion
  • The Social Dilemma - Examines AI's role in social media
  • Lo and Behold, Reveries of the Connected World by Werner Herzog
